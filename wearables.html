Wearables Concept

FeelWellTogether is a revolutionary new tool for inter-personal contacts in busy environments. The FeelWellTogether measures elements such as heart rate and galvanic skin response in people to determine who is at ease in a situation using sensors placed on the persons inner arm, and who is feeling uncomfortable. When a person approaches another person who is also using FeelWellTogether the two will exchange information and let either party know how they are feeling. This allows people to gauge easier if the other party want to talk, and also how relaxed they are with said conversation.
Outside of this the FeelWellTogether also allows people who feel insecure opt into a buddy program, were they can give preferences to the FeelWellTogether about who they want to talk to when they are in which mood state. Through this people who might feel uneasy around large groups could try to be paired with someone who is more at ease in these situations. Which could help them.
Furthermore the FeelWellTogether allows people who are talking with other people to help them understand how the other person is feeling about said topic and if they accept or reject the topic currently being discussed. This could prove useful during large conversation where not everyone might be on the same wavelength. This tool allows these groups to have common topics to talk about which donâ€™t make some people feel uneasy.
Lastly the FeelWellTogether also doubles as a medical control, where if it detects any sudden abnormalities within its user it sends a message to check up on its host. The host then has to confirm that it is still functioning enough to acknowledge its message and cancel the distress call. Firstly the FeelWellTogether would notify a nearby user to check up on its host, otherwise it sends an emergency call to the local authorities. Another feature within this could be an alcohol lock, that locks or unlocks based on the amount of alcohol in the hosts sweat.

Research

Light Flex

Light Flex focuses on making users more visible in low-light situations by incorporating lights into clothing without compromising aesthetics or comfort. It is fully washable and bendable into any shape to be integrated into clothing.



Made by Light Flex Technology.

Unlimited Hand

Unlimited Hand is technology developed by Happy Hacking Life to provide tactile feedback from virtual objects using whilst also allowing direct interaction. This is done via sensors measuring muscle activity in the lower forearm to detect motions. Tactile feedback is achieved via an EMS which discharges controlled electric signals to fake the hands and fingers to think they have real interaction and control.



Made by Happy Hacking Life.
